07 — Board Summary
What happened, what it means
Key Takeaways
  • Spent 4.8% more, earned 6.3% less. ROAS fell from 3.10x to 2.77x — the biggest weekly ROAS drop in our testing. Extra morning spend isn't converting as efficiently without sustained PM coverage.
  • Monday was the standout. Only day with less spend AND more sales than last week. ROAS improved to 3.13x. Ads ran cleanly through 3 PM with no dead zones before noon.
  • Wednesday had the best PM coverage — 12 PM active ($1,055), sustained 2–6 PM spending. This pattern consistently delivers the strongest afternoon results across all weeks tested.
  • Thursday collapsed again. Budget died at 12 PM ($52), revived at 1 PM, then died again at 4 PM. Lost $4,187 in ad sales vs same day last week and $6,763 vs the day before.
  • Evening window lost. W2 generated $1,743 in evening sales (6–9 PM). W3 had only $277. Without the 5 PM cap sustaining into evening, that revenue disappears.
  • The 1 PM dead zone persists. Tuesday ($10), Wednesday ($11) — nearly zero spend at 1 PM on 3 of 4 days. The gap between noon budget exhaustion and the 2 PM cap continues to cost $2K–$5K per day in lost sales.
⚡ Recommendation

The data across 3 weeks is consistent: Wednesday's pattern works. Active 12 PM spend + 2 PM cap + sustained through 5–6 PM delivers the best results. The priority is closing the 1 PM gap with a 12:30 PM cap ✦ and ensuring the 5 PM cap is large enough to sustain evening activity.
