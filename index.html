08 — Board Summary
PM improving, but overall ROAS dipped
Key Takeaways
  • Overall: spent 2.4% more, earned 1.0% less. ROAS slipped from 2.99x to 2.89x. The extra spend isn't fully converting yet — but the PM window is the bright spot.
  • PM sales jumped +13.0% on nearly flat PM spend — $6,768 more in afternoon sales. The 12 PM improvement ($5,542 → $12,305) is the single biggest hourly gain.
  • Friday was the biggest turnaround. Last week: 5-hour PM blackout, $38.8K sales, 2.91x. This week: $43.1K sales (+$4,367), 2.98x ROAS. Active 12–2 PM made the difference.
  • Thursday and Wednesday dragged the week down. Thu lost $7,387 in sales vs last week (worst single-day drop), Wed lost $5,635. Both had higher spend but lower sales — the afternoon cap timing didn't hold.
  • Evening window was lost. W1 had $5,219 in evening sales. W2 had just $277. Without the 5 PM cap sustaining spend past 6 PM, this revenue disappears.
  • Not resetting to $0 at midnight (Thu/Fri) gave Friday more noon budget — $1,355 at 12 PM and $4,813 in 12 PM sales. Worth testing deliberately as a strategy for days where morning spend is aggressive.
